The Childhood of Fovero, Hugo, and Kason

A long time ago, in a far away land where animals could walk and talk like humans, there lived a small kit-fox named Fovero. She was an energetic child, bright eyed and bushy tailed, both in spirit and in the most literal sense. She was somewhat mischievous, hopping over fences and playing pranks on people, but she was honest and always ready to apologize, earning her reputation in the village as troublesome but sweet child.

She was good friends with a wolf-pup named Kason. They played together often, imagining themselves going on adventures. She also sometimes played with a mountain lion-cub named Hugo, though Kason never joined in, for his mother told him never to trust felines. Fovero did not play with Hugo that often, he was very shy, and she preferred Kason a little more.

As time moved forward and Fovero grew a little, she found Kason to be a bit difficult; he had a short temper, and he could be very hurtful from time to time, though Fovero's backtalk never helped the situation. When she was hurt, she found Hugo to be very kind and compassionate, and because of this she grew closer to Hugo. Kason always got around to apologizing, so Fovero never stopped being friends with him. One argument in particular was about Kason's mistrust of Hugo. Kason never got along with Hugo, until he was properly introduced by Fovero. After much difficulty, Kason saw how kind Hugo was and came to accept him. From then on, Fovero, Hugo, and Kason all hung out together and had good times.

The Arrival of Mysterious Aleah

They all kept growing together, and now they were on the cusp of adulthood. something strange happened. A border collie rode in on horse, and she was somber, not saying much. She only asked for a place to stay and to eat, and when Fovero offered her hospitality, the border collie remained silent, nodding her head as if to nervously say, 'thank you'. When Fovero's family began to eat, They asked the border collie's name. The border collie replied, "Aleah", and Aleah silently continued eating. Fovero then asked where she came from. At this question, Aleah stopped eating and misery was all over her face. "I'm sorry", said Fovero. They finished eating, and got ready for bed. Fovero was curious about Aleah, but, not wanting to pry, she asked nothing and went to bed.

When the sun came up, Hugo visited them. He was intrigued when he saw Aleah, not knowing who she was or where she was from, so Fovero introduced her to him. Aleah was given food for her travel, and she went to pack up and leave. "I'm surprised you didn't know she was here", said Fovero. "I was busy with other things", said Hugo. He looked at Aleah, "Is she alright by the way?". "I'm not so sure", said Fovero, "Yesterday, she looked so sad, and I think she might be lost". Hugo went for Aleah and talked to her. "Fovero and I think you're lost," said Hugo, "and we can offer you a place to stay, if you need." Aleah nervously accepted.

During the first weeks of Aleah's stay, she came to Hugo often, finding comfort in his presence. She told Hugo about her worries, and Hugo listened to her. He learned in time that she was forced to leave her town, and he saw that she was very distressed by this, so he asked nothing of it, fearing that it would only upset her to question further. Aleah was always so somber and worrisome, and it broke Hugo's heart. He too had his own sorrows to share with Aleah, and they became good friends.

Aleah became a big subject of conversation among the townspeople. Kason himself was skeptical of Aleah for a few days, but he got used to her. He only needed to know she wasn't a robber, or worse. Aleah wasn't a big fan of being so talked about. Luckily, interest in her seemed to die down as the townspeople found something new to gossip about.

The King’s Death and it’s Consequences

The king was old and everyone knew this. Traditionally, the king held a court where all the dukes brought five representatives from their land, and discussed politics, but over the years, this was helping nobody. The Duke of Nordlichtestadt, Averdolf the Raven, was especially off put by the council, as he could never get approval for his plans for what he called "purification of species" and was called a madman by the Duke of the south. He stormed out of the castle one day, which made the king angry. A small army was sent to Nordlichtestadt to clear up the issue, but when they arrived a short battle was fought. The battle was concluded when a bird scout of the royal eye sent a message that declared the king dead. Since the king had failed to produce an heir, his last request was for all the dukes to hold one last council to choose his successor. when all of them showed up, Averdolf declared war to all of them, The rest all declared war back on him, and then each other. He knew that while he was powerful, he was not powerful enough to conquer all of them, but he was smart enough. Since he was a raven, he won over the respect of the royal eye (after all they were all birds); an order was sent out by a royal eye to his top general to invade his neighbor to the east. His conquest began before he even arrived back at his castle.

Of the reuniting of Aleah and Theophania

When Aleah, Hugo, Kason, and Fovero arrived at the camp ground of the refugees, Aleah saw something familiar out of the corner of her eye. She looked again though the crowd and, for the briefest of seconds, saw the familiar face again. Why was it familiar? She was drawn to the face and when she pondered over what she saw, it dawned on her that she saw the face of none other than Theophania.

"how could that be?" she whispered to herself. She knew Theophania's fate and all the grief it caused her; the grief struck her heart again. She felt a great longing to see that face, and so she set off into the crowd without Hugo's, Fovero's, or Kason's knowledge.

She searched, though it was nearly impossible to find anyone among the crowd. She bumped into people, and witnessed many others grieving the loss of their homes, or their family. she wanted to turn back now; she was getting anxious! She tried to find Fovero or Hugo, but she was completely lost. She was nearing panic, but she saw the familiar face once more, and that face stared back. The face was beautiful, just as Aleah remembered, and the eyes spoke of a great longing. It was not just the trick of the eye, this was Theophania. They walked towards each other. Aleah began to cry, for she missed Theophania. She leaped and hugged her so tight, as if never to let go. Eventually She did let go, and she wanted to ask many questions.

The throwing of stones

Aleah and Theophania were dragged by the angry crowd. They where thrown to the ground by the mob. The voices yelled horribly and Aleah feared for her life. An apple was thrown at them, then a small rock, which hit Aleah, and then someone hurled a stone at Theophania. She collapsed to the ground and Aleah leaped for her. She cried and backed away with Theophania's unconscious body. Another stone was hurled ad she was hit. It stung and she yelled; a little blood came out. The fearful cry of Hugo was heard.

"Stop it! Please!", he yelled in anguish, "Stop!"
